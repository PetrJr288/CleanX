How to Properly Care for Ankara & Traditional Fabrics

Feb 20, 2026 5 min read CleanX Team
HomeBlogAnkara & Traditional Fabrics

Traditional Nigerian fabrics — ankara, aso-oke, George fabric, adire, and isi-agu — are not only beautiful expressions of culture and identity, they are also significant investments. Caring for them incorrectly results in faded colours, shrinkage, damaged embroidery, and a shortened lifespan. This guide shares everything you need to know to keep your traditional fabrics looking rich, vibrant, and new.

Caring for Ankara (African Wax Print)

Ankara's bold, vibrant prints are achieved using a wax-resist dyeing process that makes the colours rich but also susceptible to fading if not cared for properly.

Hand Wash First

Always hand wash new ankara separately for the first few washes to allow excess dye to bleed off without affecting other garments.

Cold Water Only

Hot water causes the wax in ankara to melt, dulling the print and causing the fabric to lose its characteristic stiffness.

Mild Detergent

Choose a gentle, pH-neutral detergent. Avoid detergents with bleach or optical brighteners which strip colour rapidly.

Dry in Shade

Hang ankara inside out in a shaded, well-ventilated area. Direct sunlight is the most common cause of premature fading.

Pro Tip — Preserve the Starch

Quality ankara has a natural stiffness from wax. To preserve it, wash in cool water and avoid excessive rinsing. A light starch spray before ironing will restore its structure if it softens.

Caring for Aso-Oke

Aso-oke is a hand-woven prestige fabric used for ceremonies and special occasions. It comes in three main types: ẹtu (dark indigo), sanyan (light brown), and alari (bright red). Each requires careful handling.

  • Aso-oke should almost always be dry cleaned — home washing risks damaging the weave structure
  • If hand washing is necessary, use extremely cold water and the gentlest possible motion
  • Never wring or twist aso-oke — the woven strips can come apart under tension
  • Lay flat to dry on a clean surface, reshaping gently as it dries
  • Iron on low heat with a pressing cloth between the iron and the fabric
Handle with Extreme Care

Aso-oke is a prestige fabric and can be very expensive. If in any doubt, always take it to a professional dry cleaner experienced with traditional Nigerian fabrics.

Caring for George Fabric

George fabric, particularly Indian George, is a popular choice across southern Nigeria for ceremonial attire. It is typically a heavy, rich fabric with a smooth finish, often featuring embroidery or beading.

  • Always dry clean embellished or beaded George to protect the decorations
  • For plain George, hand wash in cold water with a very small amount of mild detergent
  • Do not soak for long — 5 to 10 minutes maximum to prevent colour bleeding
  • Roll in a dry towel to remove excess water — never wring
  • Iron on a low, silk-safe setting while slightly damp, always on the reverse side

Caring for Adire and Batik

Adire is a Nigerian hand-dyed fabric using resist-dyeing technique, creating beautiful indigo blue patterns. Both adire and batik bleed colour freely and require special treatment.

  • Wash adire and batik separately — they bleed heavily, especially when new
  • Add a tablespoon of white vinegar to cold rinse water to help set colour and reduce bleeding
  • Use the absolute minimum amount of gentle detergent
  • Never use hot water — it aggressively releases dye from adire fabric
  • Do not tumble dry — hang in shade immediately after washing

Storing Traditional Fabrics Correctly

How you store your traditional garments between wears is just as important as how you wash them. Poor storage leads to discolouration, permanent creasing, mildew, and insect damage.

Fabric Storage Method Avoid
Ankara Fold and store flat in a cool, dry drawer Direct sunlight, plastic bags
Aso-Oke Wrap in acid-free tissue, store in a cotton bag Plastic storage, damp areas
George Hang with padded hangers or fold flat Wire hangers, crushing under heavy items
Adire / Batik Fold with tissue between layers Storing damp, strong light exposure
Isi-agu / Velvet Hang with fabric face inward Folding (causes permanent crush marks)
