How to Sort Your Laundry the Right Way

Feb 5, 2026 4 min read CleanX Team
HomeBlogSorting Laundry

Sorting laundry before washing is one of the simplest yet most important habits you can develop. It takes just a few extra minutes but protects your clothes from colour bleeding, shrinkage, fabric damage, and uneven cleaning. Most laundry disasters — a white shirt turned pink, a shrunk sweater — are entirely preventable with correct sorting. Here is how to do it the right way.

Why Sorting Laundry Matters

Many people throw all their clothes into a single wash because it seems faster. However, mixing incompatible items causes several problems that shorten the lifespan of your garments and can ruin favourites permanently.

  • Dark fabrics bleed dye onto light-coloured items, permanently staining them
  • Delicate fabrics get damaged when washed with heavy items like jeans and towels
  • Different fabrics require different water temperatures — mixing them forces a compromise that cleans nothing optimally
  • Zippers, hooks, and embellishments snag and tear softer fabrics in the same load

Step 1 — Sort by Colour

The most fundamental sorting step is separating laundry by colour. Create these four basic groups:

W

Whites

White shirts, underwear, socks, and bed sheets. Can be washed with warm water and whitening detergent.

L

Lights & Pastels

Light pinks, yellows, creams, and pale blues. Wash together safely on a warm or cool cycle.

D

Darks

Black, navy, dark grey, dark green. Wash in cold water to preserve colour and prevent fading.

B

Brights

Vibrant reds, oranges, strong colours. New bright items should be washed separately for the first few washes.

New Items Alert

Always wash new garments — especially brightly coloured or dark items — separately for the first two or three washes. New fabrics often bleed excess dye that can permanently stain other garments.

Step 2 — Sort by Fabric Type

After sorting by colour, further separate by fabric type. Different fabrics have different washing requirements, and mixing them forces compromises that serve none of them well.

Fabric Group Examples Wash Setting Water Temp
Delicates Silk, lace, chiffon Hand wash / delicate cycle Cold
Synthetics Polyester, nylon, acrylic Gentle / synthetic cycle Cool (30°C)
Cotton Everyday T-shirts, underwear, towels Normal cycle 40°C–60°C
Heavy Items Jeans, denim, canvas Heavy duty cycle 40°C
Woolens / Knits Sweaters, knitwear Wool / hand wash Cold (30°C max)
Bedding & Towels Sheets, bath towels Normal / hot cycle 60°C

Step 3 — Separate by Soil Level

Even within the same colour and fabric group, heavily soiled items should be separated from lightly soiled ones. Washing heavily soiled clothes with lightly soiled wear means dirty water can re-deposit onto the cleaner clothes.

  • Pre-soak or pre-treat heavily soiled items separately before adding to the main wash
  • Wash heavily soiled items on their own cycle at a higher temperature if the fabric permits
  • Use a higher detergent quantity only for the heavily soiled load, not the entire wash

Pre-Wash Checklist

Before loading your sorted laundry, run through this quick checklist to avoid common mistakes:

  • Empty all pockets — tissues, coins, and receipts cause damage and mess in the wash
  • Close all zippers and fasten all hooks to prevent snagging other fabrics
  • Turn jeans and dark garments inside out to preserve colour and protect finishes
  • Place delicate items in mesh laundry bags before adding to the machine
  • Check care labels on any unfamiliar items before adding them to a load
  • Remove belts, detachable embellishments, or tie-on decorations before washing
