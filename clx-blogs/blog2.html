10 Easy and Effective Methods to Remove Oil Stains

Nov 23, 2025 7 min read CleanX Team
HomeBlogOil Stain Removal

Oil stains are among the most frustrating stains to deal with. Whether it's cooking oil, engine grease, palm oil, or salad dressing, these stains can quickly ruin your favourite garments if not treated correctly and promptly. With the right methods, even stubborn oil stains can be fully removed.

1. Act Immediately — Blot, Don't Rub

The single most important thing when an oil stain occurs is to act fast. The longer oil sits on fabric, the deeper it penetrates. Use a clean cloth or paper towel to blot the excess oil from the surface immediately.

Never Rub the Stain

Rubbing spreads the oil and pushes it deeper into the fibres. Always blot gently from the outside edge inward.

2. Apply Baking Soda or Cornstarch

After blotting, sprinkle a generous layer of baking soda or cornstarch over the stain. These powders draw the remaining oil out of the fabric. Leave for at least 30 minutes — or overnight for older stains — then brush away gently.

3. Dish Soap — A Powerful Degreaser

Dish soap is formulated to cut through grease, making it one of the most effective household remedies. Apply a few drops directly onto the stain, work it in gently, let it sit for 5–10 minutes, then rinse with warm water.

Best Practice

Choose a clear dish soap to avoid adding dye to your fabric. Dawn, Fairy, or any degreasing dish liquid works excellently.

4. WD-40 for Set-In Oil Stains

WD-40 can help lift set-in oil stains by breaking the bond between dried oil and fabric fibres. Spray a small amount onto the stain, let it sit for 20 minutes, then apply dish soap over the top before washing normally.

5. White Chalk

Rub white chalk generously over the stain and leave for several minutes. The chalk absorbs the oil. Brush it off and repeat as needed before laundering. Works especially well on light-coloured fabrics.

6. Shampoo or Conditioner

Clarifying shampoo contains surfactants that break down oils effectively. Apply a small amount to the stain, work it in, leave for 10 minutes, then rinse. This is gentle enough for delicate fabrics.

7. Aloe Vera Gel

Aloe vera's natural enzymes help break down oil molecules. Apply fresh aloe vera gel directly to the stain, leave for 30 minutes, then wash as normal. An excellent eco-friendly alternative.

8. Commercial Stain Removers

Products like Vanish or Ace are formulated to tackle oil-based stains. Apply as directed, pretreat before washing, and always test on an inconspicuous area first.

9. Wash at the Hottest Safe Temperature

After pretreating, wash in the hottest water temperature the care label permits. Higher temperatures help flush out oil residue.

Important

Do not put a stained garment in the tumble dryer before confirming the stain is gone. Dryer heat sets oil stains permanently.
